League Suspends Chicago's Swanson
Thursday, September 18th , 2025
The Chicago Cubs were hoping for a more lenient ruling from the league office, but they didn't get it. In yesterday's game at Great American Ball Park against the Cincinnati Reds, Cubs shortstop Dansby Swanson was ejected by the home plate umpire after he vociferously questioned a third-strike call.

The commissioner's office issued a 4-game suspension, saying the league would not tolerate a player behaving in that manner. The disgruntled Swanson refused to leave the field and kicked dirt on the umpire's shoes. His manager tried to keep him away from the umpire and physically had to remove him to the dugout with the help of the two other coaches.

Reached for comment after getting the suspension notice, Swanson said he was too disgusted to talk about it.

This season, Swanson is swinging the bat at a .222 pace. He has hit 11 home runs and scored 46 times, while driving in 47. His on-base percentage is .300 and he has played in 143 games.
