MWL Reliever of the Year Winner Named
Thursday, October 16th , 2025
West Michigan Whitecaps closer CJ Weins said he loves to come into the game when the outcome is in jeopardy.

He certainly did a good job of impacting his team's outcomes this season, as he has been named the winner of the 2025 Reliever of the Year for the Midwest League.

"There's no better feeling than running in from the bullpen with the game on the line and your heart pumping," Weins said.

On the season, the Lockport, Illinois native had 4 saves in 36 relief appearances, 95 strikeouts in 63.1 innings and a record of 4 wins and 3 losses to go with his 3.41 ERA.

He received 12 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Reynaldo Yean of the Great Lakes Loons finished second in voting, while Ethan Sloan of the West Michigan Whitecaps finished third.
