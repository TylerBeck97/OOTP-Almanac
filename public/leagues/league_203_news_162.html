St. Louis Skipper Marmol Blasts Media Over Bad Press
Monday, April 21st , 2025
Cardinals manager, Oliver Marmol, had some choice words for the St. Louis media today. "Whatever happened to journalistic integrity?" the Cardinals skipper asked during a live radio interview. "No one cares about reporting the facts anymore. It's all about sensationalism these days. All you guys care about is selling newspapers and driving up ratings. And I'm getting pretty damn sick of it." Marmol seemed to be referring to recent stories that have suggested the Cardinals struggles as of late have been the result of off-field distractions.

One article, which accuses Miles Mikolas of numerous "extracurricular indiscretions", has been a particularly sore subject for Marmol. "What players do on their own time is their business, but to imply that any of my players aren't giving it their all on the field is absolutely ridiculous," Marmol said, "and to make these kinds of accusations against someone without any proof is just plain irresponsible!"

Team officials had no comment when asked about Marmol's remarks.
