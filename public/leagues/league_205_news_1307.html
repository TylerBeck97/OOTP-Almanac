Carrigg Extends Hit Streak to 20 Games
Tuesday, July 15th , 2025
The streak is alive and well -- at least for today.

Cole Carrigg did it again, bashing 2 hits in the contest at Rio Grande Credit Union Field at Isotopes Park. For the last 20 games in a row, the 23-year-old center fielder has collected at least one hit. His efforts helped the Albuquerque Isotopes beat the Tacoma Rainiers, 9-5.

"I'm happy I kept the streak going," Carrigg told a standing-room-only crowd of reporters gathered in the Albuquerque clubhouse. "Honestly though, I'm looking forward to going home and not thinking about this for the rest of the day."
Cole Carrigg struck out in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Emerson Hancock in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, struck out in the 7th and grounded out in the 8th.

Thus far this year, Carrigg is hitting .328 with 17 home runs, 42 RBIs and 42 runs scored.
