Espinoza Goes Cycling
Friday, May 16th , 2025
Leonel Espinoza accomplished one of the rarer feats in baseball when he hit for the cycle against the Columbus Clingstones in the 11-6 Knoxville Smokies win at Smokies Stadium.

Afterward, Espinoza acknowledged that his accomplishment was special, but said he was more interested in helping the Smokies win.

"Maybe one day I'll look back on this with more meaning, but right now I just want to go out tomorrow and put some good swings on the ball and help my club win another game," Espinoza said.

On his historic day, he went 4-for-5 with 5 runs batted in and 3 runs scored -- and also got a spot in the history books.

Leonel Espinoza hit a solo-shot off Herick Hernandez in the 1st, hit an RBI double in the 3rd, hit a three-run triple in the 4th, flied out in the 6th and singled in the 7th.

This season, Espinoza has posted a batting average of .408 with 29 hits in 71 at-bats, 1 home run and 12 RBIs. He has also scored 9 runs, walked 8 times and compiled a .482 OBP.
