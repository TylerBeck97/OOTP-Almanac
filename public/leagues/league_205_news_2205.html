Sacramento's Villar Suspended 2 Games
Monday, September 15th , 2025
The Sacramento River Cats were hoping for a more lenient ruling from the league office, but they didn't get it. In yesterday's game at Dell Diamond against the Round Rock Express, River Cats first baseman David Villar was ejected by the home plate umpire after he vociferously questioned a third-strike call.

The commissioner's office issued a 2-game suspension, saying the league would not tolerate a player behaving in that manner. The disgruntled Villar refused to leave the field and kicked dirt on the umpire's shoes. His manager tried to keep him away from the umpire and physically had to remove him to the dugout with the help of the two other coaches.

Reached for comment after getting the suspension notice, Villar said he was too disgusted to talk about it.

This year, Villar is batting .348 with 7 home runs and 26 RBIs. In 34 games he has scored 28 times.
