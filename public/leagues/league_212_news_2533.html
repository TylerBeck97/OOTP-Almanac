No Easy Feat: Pitcher Wins CAL Most Valuable Player Award
Thursday, October 23rd , 2025
For fans of the Modesto Nuts, there was certainly no doubting the value of Shaddon Peavyhouse this season.

Now the 27-year-old pitcher has an award to show just how valuable he was, as he's been named the 2025 California League Most Valuable Player Award winner.

His remarkable campaign saw him put up a 6-2 record. In 10 starts he piled up 74.2 innings, had 107 strikeouts with 18 walks and a 2.77 ERA. Peavyhouse held opposing hitters to a .225 batting average and compiled a 3.0 WAR.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 8. Angel Diaz of the Rancho Cucamonga Quakes finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Aldalay Kolokie of the Fresno Grizzlies finished third.
