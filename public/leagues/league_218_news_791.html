League Gives Cason 2-Game Suspension
Sunday, June 15th , 2025
The Boston (FCL) Red Sox were hoping for a more lenient ruling from the league office, but they didn't get it. In yesterday's game at JetBlue Park against the Toronto (FCL) Blue Jays, Red Sox starting pitcher Conrad Cason was ejected by the home plate umpire after he vociferously questioned a third-strike call.

The commissioner's office issued a 2-game suspension, saying the league would not tolerate a player behaving in that manner. The disgruntled Cason refused to leave the field and kicked dirt on the umpire's shoes. His manager tried to keep him away from the umpire and physically had to remove him to the dugout with the help of the two other coaches.

Reached for comment after getting the suspension notice, Cason said he was too disgusted to talk about it.
