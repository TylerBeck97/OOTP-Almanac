Super Season, Levenson Wins Most Valuable Player Award
Monday, October 20th , 2025
The Midwest League has selected its Most Valuable Player Award for 2025 and the honor went to Zach Levenson, Peoria's fine right fielder.

He starred this season with a .297 batting average, 90 hits, 17 home runs, 68 RBIs, .419 OBP and 60 runs scored.

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 12. Eduardo Quintero of the Great Lakes Loons finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Franyerber Montilla of the West Michigan Whitecaps finished third.
