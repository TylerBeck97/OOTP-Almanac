Martini Heads EL Best Player List
Sunday, October 12th , 2025
Nick Martini said he felt great coming into this year and expected a productive campaign.

But did the Hartford Yard Goats first baseman ever anticipate being named the winner of the 2025 Eastern League Most Valuable Player Award?

"Not a chance, I'm 35 years old, not exactly a spring chicken out there," he said. "I always felt I had a season like this in me, but still a surprise when it happens."

On the year, Nick Martini posted a .353 batting average and a .458 on-base percentage. In 76 games he piled up 96 hits, 14 doubles, 2 triples, 14 home runs, 48 RBIs and 50 runs scored.

He received 12 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Jace Bohrofen of the New Hampshire Fisher Cats finished second in voting, while Jesus Baez of the Binghamton Rumble Ponies finished third.
