Guerrero Shows Off, Hits for Cycle
Tuesday, May 20th , 2025
Pablo Guerrero did everything he could for the Texas (ACL) Rangers -- literally.

The 18-year-old first baseman hit a single, a double, a triple and a home run for the Rangers, going for the cycle and helping them get past the Kansas City (ACL) Royals, 15-6, at Surprise Stadium.

"Yeah, I was some kind of hitter in this game," Guerrero reflected later. "It was like all I had to do was close my eyes and swing the bat."

In a game headed for the record books, Guerrero banged out 4 hits in 4 at-bats and totaled 4 RBI and 2 runs scored. Speaking with the press about the feat, he attributed it to "a lot of luck."

"A triple is so hard to hit," he said. "To just decide to get one in a game when you need it for a cycle, it's impossible. Usually something weird has to happen, the ball kicking past an outfielder or somebody falling down. There's a ton of luck in hitting for a cycle."

Pablo Guerrero hit a two-run triple in the 1st, walked in the 3rd, walked in the 4th, hit a solo-shot off Julio Gutierrez in the 6th, singled in the 7th, hit an RBI double in the 8th and was hit by a pitch in the 9th.

For the year, Guerrero is batting .426 with 7 home runs, 27 RBIs and 17 runs scored.
