Hall of Fame Welcomes Beltran
Wednesday, January 21st , 2026
The Major League Baseball Hall of Fame just added a fine center fielder and hitter to its number. The newest member of baseball's most elite group is Carlos Beltran, who retired at the age of 40.

"I always knew I was good enough to play, but the Hall of Fame was one of the few things I never even dreamed of," said Beltran at his induction ceremony. "For me to focus a lot on the individual, that's not the way I learned to play the game. Baseball is a team game. You win as a team, you lose as team. You also do so many things together, but it is not an 'I' thing."

During his illustrious career, Beltran had 435 home runs among his 2725 hits, while batting .279. He played in 2586 games, scored 1582 runs and knocked in 1587 runs.
