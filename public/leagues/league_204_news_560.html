Cordero Cracks 3 HRs for Rochester
Thursday, May 29th , 2025
Rochester Red Wings right fielder Franchy Cordero put on a dazzling display of power at Frontier Field against the St. Paul Saints. In the 19-5 Red Wings victory, Cordero penned his name into the record books, slamming a trio of home runs in one of baseball's finest single-day efforts.

"He can hit them out anywhere, right, center, left, it doesn't matter," St. Paul manager Toby Gardenhire said. "He's super strong and stays on the ball so well."

Along with his three home runs, the 30-year-old had 5 hits in 6 at-bats, 8 RBIs and 4 runs scored.

"Life will always throw you curves, just keep fouling them off," Cordero said after the game, when asked about his hitting philosophy. "The right pitch will come, and when it does, be prepared to run the bases. Or trot around them, like I did today."

Franchy Cordero hit a two-run home run off Zebby Matthews in the 1st, singled in the 2nd, hit a three-run home run off Randy Dobnak in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Pierson Ohl in the 5th, hit an RBI single in the 6th and struck out in the 8th.

This year, Cordero is batting .351 with 59 hits, 20 home runs, 56 RBIs, 31 walks, .455 OBP and has scored 45 runs.
