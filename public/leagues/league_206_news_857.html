5-for-5 Game for Tolentino
Tuesday, June 17th , 2025
After a 5-hit effort to lead Akron's 12-5 victory over the Binghamton Rumble Ponies, third baseman Milan Tolentino told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Eastern League-level is really something special."

The 23-year-old player earned his accolades at Canal Park with a spectacular 5-for-5 game against the Rumble Ponies.

"His swing is getting there," said RubberDucks skipper Greg DiCenzo, who spoke glowingly of Tolentino after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Milan did that to perfection."

Milan Tolentino singled in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, singled in the 5th, singled in the 7th and hit a two-run single in the 7th.

So far on the season, Tolentino is hitting .259 with 1 home run, 8 RBIs and 7 runs scored. He has 15 hits and played in 17 games.
