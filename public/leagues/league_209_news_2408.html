Slade Caldwell Chosen NWL's #1 Player
Monday, October 6th , 2025
The future looks extremely bright for Slade Caldwell. At the age of 19, many players are still trying to find their way in Northwest League.

Caldwell has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Northwest League Most Valuable Player Award.

"I didn't expect to hit .304 this year, that's for sure," the left fielder said.

The Hillsboro Hops star also put up a .388 on-base percentage this year, with 111 hits, 28 doubles, 2 triples, 12 home runs, 36 RBIs and 64 runs scored.

He received 6 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Maui Ahuna of the Eugene Emeralds finished second in voting, while Robert Calaz of the Spokane Indians finished third.
