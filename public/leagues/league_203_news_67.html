2-Game Suspensions for McCutchen-Barlow Fight
Friday, April 11th , 2025
Baseball confrontations occasionally get intense and sometimes that tension becomes too much and boils over into more than just words. This time it happened to Pittsburgh's Andrew McCutchen and Cincinnati's Scott Barlow. The two were the main participants in a fight that cleared the benches. Today both were suspended by the league for 2 games for their aggressive actions.

"I hope things settle down now," the manager of the Reds said later. "There's no reason for us to be locked in a cycle of revenge. We all need to focus on baseball instead."

On the year, McCutchen is hitting .143 with 7 hits, no home runs, 2 RBIs, .246 OBP and has scored 2 runs.

Barlow has 5 saves with a 1-0 season record and a 4.50 ERA. He also has 7 relief appearances, thrown 6 innings and struck out 10.
