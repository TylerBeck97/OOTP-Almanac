River Cats Chill Carrigg Hot Streak
Sunday, July 20th , 2025
It was a doubly miserable day for Cole Carrigg of the Albuquerque Isotopes, as he saw his 20-game hitting streak end and his team lose, 3-1, to the Sacramento River Cats at Sutter Health Park. The 23-year-old center fielder was held hitless in 3 at-bats by the River Cats.

"Anyone on a 20-game hitting streak is going to be a tough out," Sacramento manager Dave Brundage said later. "But we had a plan and we were careful about it, and got it done."

For the season, Carrigg has hit .323 with 17 home runs and 42 RBIs. He has played in 46 games and scored 42 times.
