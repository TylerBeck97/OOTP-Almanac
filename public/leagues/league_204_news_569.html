Hinds Sizzles -- Smokes Cubs for 5 Hits
Friday, May 30th , 2025
After a 5-hit effort to lead Louisville's 19-1 victory over the Iowa Cubs, left fielder Rece Hinds told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the International League-level is really something special."

The 24-year-old player earned his accolades at Louisville Slugger Field with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Cubs.

"His swing is getting there," said Bats skipper Pat Kelly, who spoke glowingly of Hinds after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Rece did that to perfection."

Rece Hinds singled in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, flied out in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, hit a two-run home run off Darien Nunez in the 7th and hit a two-run home run off Connor Noland in the 8th.

This season, Hinds is batting at a .387 clip with 14 home runs and 35 RBIs.
