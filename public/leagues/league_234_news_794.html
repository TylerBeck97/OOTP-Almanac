After a 5-hit effort to lead Cincinnati (DSL)'s 12-5 victory over the Kansas City (DSL) Royals Ventura, third baseman Brayan Joseph told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Dominican Rookie League-level is really something special."

The 19-year-old player earned his accolades at Kansas City Royals Complex with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Royals Ventura.

"His swing is getting there," said Reds skipper Juan Ballara, who spoke glowingly of Joseph after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Brayan did that to perfection."

Brayan Joseph hit an RBI single in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, doubled in the 6th, doubled in the 7th and grounded out in the 9th.

This year, Joseph is batting .429 with 12 hits and 1 home run. He has scored 7 times and has batted in 5 runs.
